Why ArtSiren exists

When you buy a concert ticket or stream music, you're making a financial choice that benefits an artist. You deserve to know — before you pay — whether that artist has publicly called for a boycott of Israel.

This isn't about politics. It's about transparency. Every artist in our tool has voluntarily made a public statement, signed an open letter, or cancelled a show in solidarity with the boycott movement. We document it. You decide.

How we verify

Every entry is sourced from reputable, mainstream publishers. We don't cite fringe blogs or unverifiable social posts.

Mainstream media Rolling Stone, Billboard, NME, The Guardian, BBC, Al Jazeera
Official statements Artist-authored posts, interviews, and official press releases
Open letters Named signatories — Musicians for Palestine, Film Workers for Palestine, etc.
Confirmed cancellations Concert cancellations reported in the press with a stated reason

Each artist card links directly to the original source so you can verify everything independently.

Privacy first

The Chrome extension works entirely inside your browser. No data is ever collected, transmitted, or stored. We don't know who you are, what sites you visit, or which artists you look up.

The website has no tracking cookies and no third-party scripts that profile you. Our privacy policy says exactly that.

Corrections & contact

  • Email us at contact@artsiren.co with a source and we'll update or remove the entry promptly
  • We do not remove verified artists without evidence of a retraction
  • Artists who have publicly reversed their position are reclassified, not silently deleted
