Why ArtSiren exists

When you buy a concert ticket or stream music, you're making a financial choice that benefits an artist. You deserve to know — before you pay — whether that artist has publicly called for a boycott of Israel.

This isn't about politics. It's about transparency. Every artist in our database has voluntarily made a public statement, signed an open letter, or cancelled a show in solidarity with the boycott movement. We document it. You decide.

How we verify

Every entry is sourced from reputable, mainstream publishers. We don't cite fringe blogs or unverifiable social posts.

  • Major outlets: Rolling Stone, Billboard, NME, The Guardian, Al Jazeera, BBC
  • Official artist statements and press releases
  • Open letters with named signatories (Musicians for Palestine, Film Workers for Palestine, etc.)
  • Confirmed concert cancellations reported in the press

Each artist card links directly to the original source so you can verify everything independently.

Alert levels

Artists are classified by the strength of their position, not our interpretation of it.

CRITICAL

Publicly called to boycott Israel, signed a formal boycott pledge, or cancelled shows specifically in solidarity.

HIGH

Expressed strong solidarity with the boycott movement or signed relevant open letters.

LOW

Made public statements in support of Palestinian rights, without explicitly joining a boycott.

Privacy first

The Chrome extension works entirely inside your browser. No data is ever collected, transmitted, or stored. We don't know who you are, what sites you visit, or which artists you look up.

The website has no tracking, no analytics cookies, and no third-party scripts that profile you. Our privacy policy is one page and says exactly that.

Corrections & contact

We take accuracy seriously. If any information is incorrect or outdated, contact us with a source and we'll update or remove the entry promptly.

  • Email: contact@artsiren.co
  • We do not remove verified artists without evidence of a retraction
  • Artists who have publicly reversed their position are reclassified, not silently deleted
